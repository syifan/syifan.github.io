I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at William & Mary since Fall 2020. I received my Ph.D. degree from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Northeastern University in 2020 under the supervision of David Kaeli. My research interests lie in GPU architecture, performance evaluation, and performance modeling.

I am always looking for Ph.D. students to join my research lab. Admitted Ph.D. students will be fully funded. Master or undergraduate students are also welcome to work in my research lab to gain research experiences. Please send me an email if you are interested in working with me.

Research Directions

Computer Architecture Simulation

Simulators are critical infrastructures that enable pre-silicon concept proofing for novel computer architecture designs. I have been the leading designer and developer of the Akita simulator framework and MGPUSim simulator. Akita is a general, flexible, parallel, high-performance simulation framework that enables rapic hardware prototyping. MGPUSim is the first GPU simulator that is capable of simulating large-scale multi-GPU systems.
